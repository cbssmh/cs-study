# 1. Title



Forget AMD: Amazon Declares War on Nvidia by Selling Its Own AI Chips



# 2. Source



- Author / Organization: Rich Duprey / 24/7 Wall St.

- Link: https://247wallst.com/

- Date: 2026-06-19



# 3. One-line Summary



Amazon is expanding its custom AI chip strategy beyond AWS infrastructure, positioning Trainium as a potential commercial alternative to Nvidia and turning the AI industry's largest customer into one of its most credible competitors. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}



# 4. Key Points



- Amazon is reportedly considering selling its internally developed AI chips (Trainium, Inferentia) to external customers instead of limiting them to AWS usage. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

- CEO Andy Jassy highlighted increasing demand for AWS custom silicon as Amazon seeks to reduce dependence on third-party chip suppliers and lower AI infrastructure costs. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

- Amazon's custom chip business is estimated to be generating nearly **$50 billion in annualized revenue**, making it larger than AMD's total 2025 revenue. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

- The article places Amazon's custom chip operation behind Nvidia's data center business (~$193.7B) but ahead of several major semiconductor competitors in scale. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

- Nvidia remains dominant due to its CUDA software ecosystem and extensive developer adoption, creating significant switching costs for customers. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

- Amazon's key advantage is distribution: AWS controls roughly **28% of the global cloud infrastructure market**, giving Amazon direct access to enterprise AI customers. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

- Amazon can bundle AI chips, compute, storage, networking, and software into a single cloud offering, allowing it to compete on total cost rather than pure hardware performance. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

- AWS generated approximately **$128.7 billion in revenue**, while Amazon produced over **$139 billion in operating cash flow**, providing substantial resources for continued chip development. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

- Amazon can validate and optimize custom silicon at hyperscale inside AWS before expanding to broader commercial markets. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

- The strategic implication is that every AI workload moved from Nvidia GPUs to Amazon Trainium shifts AI infrastructure economics toward Amazon, making the hyperscaler both Nvidia's largest customer and an increasingly significant competitor. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}



# 5. Why It Matters



- **AI infrastructure value capture is moving upward**: Hyperscalers increasingly want to own not only cloud services but also the underlying silicon.

- **Custom chips are becoming a major competitive trend**: Amazon, Google (TPU), and Microsoft-backed chip initiatives all aim to reduce dependence on Nvidia.

- **Nvidia's risk is concentration among hyperscalers**: Its largest customers are simultaneously investing billions to build alternatives.

- **AWS gains leverage even without surpassing Nvidia technically**: Lower-cost AI services may be sufficient for many enterprise workloads.
